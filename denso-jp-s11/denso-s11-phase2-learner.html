て-imasu — What's Happening Right Now
You saw this in Session 9. Now you learn it properly.
The Pattern
[verb-て] + います (imasu)

Describes an action that is currently happening or a state that is ongoing.

Kikai wo tsukatte imasu. → I'm using the machine (right now).
Kakunin wo shite imasu. → I'm doing the check.
Mite imasu. → I'm looking at it.
Floor Examples — te-form highlighted
Request
suwatte kudasai
Please sit down.
Ongoing
suwatte imasu
I'm sitting. / They're sitting.
suwaru (U-verb, P1) → suwatte
Request
mite kudasai
Please look / check.
Ongoing
mite imasu
I'm looking at it.
miru (RU-verb) → mite
Request
shite kudasai
Please do it.
Ongoing
shite imasu
I'm doing it. / It's being done.
suru (irregular) → shite
Request
kakunin shite kudasai
Please check / verify.
Ongoing
kakunin shite imasu
I'm checking / verifying.
kakunin suru → shite (suru compound verb)
て-imasu vs て-kudasai
Tsukatte kudasai → Please use it. (request — te + kudasai)
Tsukatte imasu → I'm using it. (ongoing — te + imasu)

Same te-form, different second word, completely different meaning. The te-form is the connector.

Block D
Saying Where Things Are
You already know に for destination. Now: に for location. Same particle — this time with います or あります instead of a movement verb.
います
imasu
People and animals — living, moving things
Tanaka-san が います
Supervisor が います
Neko が います
あります
arimasu
Objects and things — non-living items
Tejun が あります
Kikai が あります
Repōto が あります
The full pattern
[person/thing] が [place] に います/あります

が marks what is there. に marks where. います/あります says it exists there.

Tanaka-san ga Station 3 ni imasu.
Tanaka-san is at Station 3.

Tejun ga tsukue no ue ni arimasu.
The procedure sheet is on the desk.
が here vs は in other sentences
When you use が with います/あります, you are pointing out specific, new information — "it is this person who is there." This is the same が from S8: が for focus, は for topic. Both are correct in different contexts — が is the natural choice when location is new information.
Quick Check — います or あります?
Floor Exchanges
You are looking for your line leader.
Worker
Yamamoto-san ga doko ni imasu ka?
山本さんがどこにいますか？
Where is Yamamoto-san?
Coworker
Station 2 ni imasu.
ステーション2にいます。
She is at Station 2.
💡 doko に imasu ka — where is [person]? Answer drops が and just gives place + imasu.
A new worker asks where the procedure sheet is.
New Worker
Tejun ga doko ni arimasu ka?
手順がどこにありますか？
Where is the procedure sheet?
Worker
Station 1 ni arimasu.
ステーション1にあります。
It is at Station 1.
💡 Same pattern, あります because tejun is a thing, not a person.

Block E
Putting It All Together
These exchanges combine て-imasu, location に, and verb chains from S9–S11.
A supervisor asks where Tanaka-san is and what he is doing.
Supervisor
Tanaka-san ga doko ni imasu ka?
Where is Tanaka-san?
Worker
Tanaka-san wa kyuukei-shitsu ni imasu. Hirugohan wo tabete imasu.
田中さんは休憩室にいます。昼ごはんを食べています。
Tanaka-san is in the break room. He is eating lunch.
💡 Capstone: location に + います, then て-imasu for what he is doing right now. は switches topic from "where" to "what doing."
You need to find the quality report before the meeting.
Worker A
Hinshitsu repōto ga doko ni arimasu ka?
Where is the quality report?
Worker B
Jimusho ni arimasu. Supervisor ga kakunin shite imasu.
事務所にあります。スーパーバイザーが確認しています。
It is in the office. The supervisor is checking it.
💡 あります (thing) + て-imasu chained. Notice が marks both the object (repōto) and the actor (supervisor).
You are explaining to a visitor where to go and who to speak to.
Worker
Jimusho ni itte, Yamamoto-san ga imasu kara, hanashite kudasai.
事務所に行って、山本さんがいますから、話してください。
Please go to the office — Yamamoto-san is there, so please speak with her.
💡 Destination に + itte (chain) + います for location + te-kudasai request. Note: kara = "because/so" — preview only, not tested today.
