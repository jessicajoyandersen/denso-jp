Slide 2 of 4 — The Unlock
⚡ You already know the te-form
Since Session 6, you've been saying mite kudasai, oshite kudasai, tsukatte kudasai. All of those are te-form + kudasai. Today you learn the rule that produces them — so you can build new ones yourself.
Use 1 — Giving a polite request or instruction
[verb-te] kudasai
mite kudasai→ please look / check (miru → mite)
shite kudasai→ please do (suru → shite)
kite kudasai→ please come (kuru → kite)
Slide 3 of 4 — New Pattern
Use 2 — Chaining two actions in order
The te-form connects two actions: do X, then do Y. The te-form goes in the middle. The second verb takes the full -masu or -mashita ending.
Pattern
[verb-te], [verb-masu/mashita]
Shigoto wo owatte, kaerimasu.
I finish work, then go home.
owaru → owatte (U-verb, -u→-tte) + kaerimasu
Piza wo tabete, biiru wo nomimashita.
I ate pizza, then drank beer.
taberu → tabete (RU-verb, drop -ru) + nomimashita (past)
Quick check — choose the correct te-form:
Item 1 · kuru
A line leader describes her morning: she came to work, then ate breakfast.
Kaisha ni ___, asagohan wo tabemashita.
Item 2 · taberu
You're describing your lunch break — you eat, then come back to the floor.
Hirugohan wo ___, modorimasu.
Slide 4 of 4 — Coming Up
Use 3 — Session 9B / S10
[verb-te] imasu
Describes an action currently happening or a state that is ongoing.
Tanoshimi ni shite imasu. → I'm looking forward to it.
You'll learn this properly in the next session — but you'll recognize it on the floor.
Summary — 3 uses of te-form
1. [verb-te] kudasai — Please do X. (request)
2. [verb-te], [verb] — Do X, then do Y. (chaining actions)
3. [verb-te] imasu — Is doing X right now. (ongoing — coming soon)

Block F
に — Where You're Going
に marks the destination with movement verbs. One particle, any movement verb.
The Pattern
[Destination] に [movement verb-te] kudasai

Station 3 ni itte kudasai. → Please go to Station 3.
Koko ni kite kudasai. → Please come here.

Itte = te-form of ikimasu (Group 1 exception: iku → itte, not iite). Kite = te-form of kuru (Group 3 irregular).
Location Words — Ko/So/A mirroring S6
RomajiJapaneseMeaning
kokoここhere — near me (mirrors kore)
sokoそこthere — near you (mirrors sore)
asokoあそこover there — away from both (mirrors are)
Station [number]ステーション[番号]Station 1, 2, 3…
Capstone Sentence — S8 + S9A Combined
Station 2 ni itte, kono tejun wo mite kudasai.
ステーション2に行って、この手順を見てください。
Please go to Station 2 and check this procedure.
itte — ikimasu te-form (exception) wo mite — S8 を + Group 2 te-form ni — destination particle
Floor Exchanges
Line leader redirects a worker to a different station.
Line Leader
Station ni ni itte kudasai.
ステーション2に行ってください。
Please go to Station 2.
Worker
Hai, wakarimashita.
Yes, understood.
💡 [destination] に itte kudasai. に marks where they're going.
Supervisor calls a worker over.
Supervisor
Chotto kite kudasai.
ちょっと来てください。
Please come here a moment.
Worker
Hai, tadaima.
はい、ただいま。
Yes, right away.
💡 Tadaima = right away / coming. Kite = te-form of kuru (Group 3 irregular).
Line leader sends a worker to check a procedure at another station.
Line Leader
Station san ni itte, kono tejun wo mite kudasai.
ステーション3に行って、この手順を見てください。
Please go to Station 3 and check this procedure.
Worker
Hai, wakarimashita. Yoroshiku onegaishimasu.
Yes, understood. I'll take care of it.
💡 Two te-forms chained: itte (go) + mite kudasai (check). に marks destination, を marks object.
