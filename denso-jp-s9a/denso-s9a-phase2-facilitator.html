The Big Picture — Why Verbs Change
Orient students before any conjugation. Today = Groups 2 and 3. Group 1 (U-verbs) is in Session 9B.
Talking points
1
Point to the three cards on their screens. "Every Japanese verb belongs to one of three groups. The group tells you what happens to the ending when you conjugate it."
2
"Group 2 — RU-verbs — is the easiest. One rule: drop ru, add the ending. We're doing that today in Block D."
3
"Group 3 is only two verbs — suru and kuru. No pattern, just memorise. Block E."

"Group 1 is the biggest group and has four sub-patterns. We'll do that in 9B — you'll already have the te-form rule down by then."

Block E — Group 3 Irregulars
Unlocks the suru/kuru conjugation widget on learner screens.
Block E
Suru + Kuru — Just Memorise
No rule. Two verbs. All four forms each. Students need these to use te-form on the floor.
Talking points
1
"These two don't follow any pattern — that's why they're called irregulars. Every language has a few. You just memorise them."
2
Suru is everywhere: shigoto wo shimasu, kakunin wo shimashita, shite kudasai. They've heard all of these. Connect explicitly.
3
Kuru → kite is the te-form of "come." That's the verb in kite kudasai from S6. Another "you already knew this" moment.
Forms to know
Dict form-masu-mashitate-form
surushimasushimashitashite
kurukimasukimashitakite
Watch for
⚠️
kite vs kitte: kite = come (kuru te-form). kitte = cut (kiru te-form, double-t). Different verbs. If it comes up, acknowledge it — don't gloss over.

Block F — に Destination + Floor Exchanges
Unlocks the capstone sentence and three floor exchange drills.
Block F
に — Destination Particle
Pulls together S8 particles, te-form, and location words into real floor sentences.
Talking points
1
"に after a place means you're going there. Station 3 ni itte kudasai — please go to Station 3." Point to the location words table.
2
Walk through the capstone sentence on their screens: "Station 2 ni itte, kono tejun wo mite kudasai." Point out each particle — ni for destination, wo for object, te-form to chain.
3
Floor exchanges: run all three as pair drills. Swap roles. Use the Hear it button if pronunciation is uncertain.
4
Close with: "Next session — 9B — you'll do U-verbs and a grammar checkpoint. Everything from today carries forward."
Capstone sentence — know it cold
Station 2 ni itte, kono tejun wo mite kudasai.
Please go to Station 2 and check this procedure.
ni — destination itte — iku te-form (exception) wo mite — S8 を + Group 2
Watch for
⚠️
itte vs iku: ikimasu → itte is a Group 1 exception (not ikiite). Flag it if it comes up — U-verb rules in 9B will explain why.
⚠️
tadaima: "right away / coming" in the second exchange. Some students know it as "I'm home" — both are correct in different contexts. If asked, briefly acknowledge.
