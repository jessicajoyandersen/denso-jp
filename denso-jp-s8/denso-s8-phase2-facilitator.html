Block B
を — The Object Particle
を marks what's being acted on. One particle, any action verb. Heaviest new content is the verb pairs — budget time for the builder.
⏱
Suggested time
6–7 min
The S6 connection is the hook — use it first
💬 Talking Points
›
Open with the S6 connection. "In Session 6 you heard: ano kikai wo tsukatte kudasai. The を was already there — you just didn't know its name. Today you learn to use it yourself."
›
Write the pattern: [object] を [verb]. Say out loud: "Kono kikai wo tsukaimasu. Tejun wo mite kudasai. Namae wo kaite kudasai." Let them hear the rhythm.
›
Show the verb pairs as fixed chunks — don't explain the conjugation. "Tsukaimasu / tsukatte kudasai. Mimasu / mite kudasai. These are pairs — learn them together." The verb table is on their screen.
›
Run the back-chain drill together, then 2–3 minutes on the sentence builder. Builder has object chips + form toggles (masu / te kudasai). Ask one or two to share what they built.
👁 Watch For
⚠️
が for を. Learners sometimes use が where を belongs — "kono kikai が tsukaimasu" (wrong — が would make the machine the subject, not the object). Correct it once: を marks the thing being acted on.
💡
If time is short after the drill, skip the builder and go straight to Block C. Block C (は vs が) is the heavier grammar and needs more time.

Block C · 8–10 min · topic vs focus · the hardest distinction
3 / 4
Block C
は vs が — Topic vs Focus
The distinction that trips learners longest. The goal isn't perfect mastery — it's enough understanding to use both correctly in common floor situations.
⏱
Suggested time
8–10 min
Hardest grammar so far — go slow, use floor examples
💬 Talking Points
›
Open by anchoring to known patterns. "You've used both already. X は Y desu — Session 5. Futtobooru が suki desu — Session 7. Both patterns were fixed. Today you'll understand why."
›
は = topic marker. "Think of は as 'as for X…' — it sets what you're talking about. Watashi は opereetaa desu = as for me, I'm an operator. Kono kikai は furui desu = as for this machine, it's old."
›
が = subject / focus. "が identifies who or what does something. It puts a spotlight. Dare が kimashita ka? → Tanaka-san が kimashita. が answers the 'who?' question." Write this on the board.
›
The contrastive は. "は can also imply contrast — 'as for me (others maybe didn't).' Watashi は yomimashita. In English this would be 'I read it' with a slight stress on 'I'. Watashi が yomimashita is more neutral — just identifying."
›
Practical floor rule: if you're answering "who?" use が. If you're setting a topic or contrasting yourself with others, use は. The side-by-side table on their screen shows this clearly — walk through it.
👁 Watch For
⚠️
Don't go too deep. Native speakers use は and が intuitively in ways that even Genki can't fully explain. The goal for this session is: (1) は sets topic, (2) が identifies, (3) dare が? → が answer. That's enough.
💡
If the group gets confused by the contrastive は, drop it and stick to the core distinction. The contrastive nuance will come with exposure — it doesn't need to land today.
💡
Good floor drill: point at someone and say "Dare が kono kikai wo tsukaimasu ka?" and have them answer with が. Then switch to は: "Tanaka-san は?" and have them complete the sentence. The contrast becomes concrete fast.
