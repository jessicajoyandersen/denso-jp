Block B — に + います/あります
Unlocks the location block — rule cards, が note, MC drill, floor exchanges.
Block B
Location に — Carried from S11
Same particle, location use. Contrast with destination に from S10.
Core teaching sequence
1
"Same に as destination — but now the verb is います or あります, not a movement verb." Write both side by side.
2
Rule of thumb — say it twice: "Person or animal → います. Object or thing → あります." Ask: Tanaka-san? Tejun? Kikai?
3
が: marks the specific thing you're pointing out. Same が as S8. New/specific info → が.
4
Pattern on board: [person/thing] が [place] に います/あります. Walk through both floor exchanges together.
Quick check answer key
QSubjectAnswerWhy
1Supervisor が jimusho に ___imasuperson
2Kikai が Station 2 に ___arimasuobject
3Tanaka-san が doko に ___ ka?imasuperson
4Tejun が Station 1 に ___arimasuobject
Watch for
⚠️
Destination vs location に: "Station 3 ni itte kudasai" (destination, movement verb) vs "Tanaka-san ga Station 3 ni imasu" (location, います). Same に, different verb. Write both.
⚠️
Kikai ga imasu: machine is not alive → あります. Catch this early.

Block C
I-Adjectives — 7 New Words
One rule: i-adjective + desu. All seven words follow it.
Vocabulary — all 7
RomajiHiraganaMeaning
nemuiねむいsleepy
hayaiはやいfast / early
osoiおそいslow / late
muzukashiiむずかしいdifficult
yasashiiやさしいeasy / kind
atarashiiあたらしいnew
furuiふるいold (things)
Teaching notes
1
Point out the pattern on the vocab grid: every card shows adjective + desu in the footer. One rule, all seven words.
2
MC is recognition — pick the right adjective for the context. Fill-in is production — type the full form with desu.
3
hayai vs osoi: call out a scenario — "The conveyor is running fast — hayai or osoi?" Quick back-and-forth to lock in the pair.
4
furui vs atarashii: both come up in kara sentences next block — reinforce now.
Watch for
⚠️
yasashii vs muzukashii: students sometimes swap these. Drill the pair: "Easy?" → yasashii. "Difficult?" → muzukashii.
⚠️
furui for people: furui is for objects/things. For old people, Japanese uses toshiyori or similar — not furui. Not tested today but worth noting if it comes up.

Block D — kara
Unlocks the kara pattern, 5 floor examples, and 3-item MC.
Block D
kara — Reason → Result
One structure. Reason first, then kara, then what happens.
Core pattern
[reason] kara, [result].
Nemui desu kara, nemasu. → I am sleepy, so I will sleep.
Nemui desu kara, nemasu.
Muzukashii desu kara, mō ichi-do hanashite kudasai.
Kikai ga furui desu kara, chekku shite kudasai.
1
"The reason always comes first in Japanese. You set up the why before you say what happens." This is the opposite of how English often works.
2
Read all 5 floor examples together. Point out: the reason is always an adjective + desu or a verb + desu before kara.
3
The most useful floor sentence: "Muzukashii desu kara, mō ichi-do hanashite kudasai." Teach this as a survival phrase — students should be able to say this immediately.
4
MC Q2 is the key check — does the reason come before kara? If students are putting kara first, address it directly.
Watch for
⚠️
Reversed order: "Nemasu kara, nemui desu" — wrong in Japanese. Result does NOT come before kara. Draw an arrow on the whiteboard: reason → kara → result.
⚠️
kara vs kara (from): kara also means "from" in time/place expressions (9-ji kara = from 9 o'clock). Different use, same word. Don't mix them today — just note it if someone asks.
