は vs が — Location Sentences
You already know に for destination. Now: に for location. Same particle — this time with います or あります instead of a movement verb.
The core difference
が — new information. Use が when you are introducing something for the first time, or pointing out specifically what is where.

は — known topic. Use は when the subject is already established and you are adding more information about it.

Tanaka-san ga Station 3 ni imasu. → Tanaka-san is at Station 3. (first mention — が introduces)
Tanaka-san wa ima isogashii desu. → Tanaka-san is busy right now. (already mentioned — は continues)
います
imasu
People and animals — living, moving things
Tanaka-san が います
Supervisor が います
Neko が います
あります
arimasu
Objects and things — non-living items
Tejun が あります
Kikai が あります
Repōto が あります
The full pattern
[person/thing] が [place] に います/あります

が marks what is there. に marks where. います/あります says it exists there.

Tanaka-san ga Station 3 ni imasu.
Tanaka-san is at Station 3.

Tejun ga tsukue no ue ni arimasu.
The procedure sheet is on the desk.
が here vs は in other sentences
When you use が with います/あります, you are pointing out specific, new information — "it is this person who is there." This is the same が from S8: が for focus, は for topic. Both are correct in different contexts — が is the natural choice when location is new information.
Quick Check — います or あります?

Na-Adjectives — A Second Type
Na-adjectives work like nouns when describing. With desu they are the same as i-adjectives — but before a noun they need な (na) between the adjective and the noun.
The pattern
[na-adj] + desu — statement: Shinsetsu desu. → Kind.
[na-adj] + na + [noun] — before a noun: shinsetsu na hito → a kind person

Compare i-adjective: hayai hito (fast person) — no extra word needed.
Na-adjective: shinsetsu na hito — な is required.

Putting It All Together
These exchanges combine は vs が, na-adjectives, location に, and kara.
A new worker asks about the supervisor.
New Worker
Supervisor wa doko ni imasu ka?
スーパーバイザーはどこにいますか？
Where is the supervisor?
Worker
Jimusho ni imasu. Shinsetsu wa hito desu kara, kikite kudasai.
事務所にいます。親切な人ですから、聞いてください。
She is in the office. She is a kind person, so please ask her.
💡 は asks about known topic (the supervisor). に location. shinsetsu na hito — な before the noun.
You report that a machine needs attention.
Worker
Station 2 ni furui kikai ga arimasu. Kikai wa daijoubu ja nai desu kara, chekku shite kudasai.
ステーション2に古い機械があります。機械は大丈夫じゃないですから、チェックしてください。
There is an old machine at Station 2. The machine is not okay, so please check it.
💡 が introduces the machine (new info). は then continues it as topic. daijoubu — na-adjective.
Explaining your work situation to a visitor.
Worker
Watashi wa DENSO de hataraite imasu. Shigoto wa taisetsu desu kara, mainichi majime ni shimasu.
私はDENSOで働いています。仕事は大切ですから、毎日真面目にします。
I work at DENSO. Work is important, so I do it seriously every day.
💡 で marks where the action (working) happens. は topic throughout. taisetsu and majime — na-adjectives.
