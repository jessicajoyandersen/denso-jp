Block B — は vs が + Location
Unlocks the は vs が explainer, rule cards, MC drill, and floor exchanges.
Block B
は vs が — The Core Difference
This is the block students struggle with most. Take your time here.
Core teaching — write this on the board
が → new information, first mention, pointing something out
は → established topic, continuing to describe something already introduced
1
"If you are introducing something for the first time — が. If it is already known and you are saying more about it — は."
2
Use the two-sentence sequence on their screens: "Tanaka-san が Station 3 に imasu" (introducing) → "Tanaka-san は ima isogashii desu" (continuing). Same person, different particles because the role changed.
3
For location sentences: が introduces what exists. は continues the topic to describe it. Both appear in the reading passage — use Block D to reinforce this.
4
Run the 4-item MC together. MC Q1 and Q2 are the key contrast (が vs は in back-to-back sentences).
MC answer key
QSubjectAnswerWhy
1Kantokusha が jimusho に ___imasuperson → います
2Kikai が Station 2 に ___arimasuobject → あります
3Tanaka-san が doko に ___ ka?imasuperson
4Tejun が Station 1 に ___arimasuobject
Watch for
⚠️
Using が everywhere: students often default to が because it feels safer. Prompt: "Have we mentioned this before? → は."
⚠️
Using は with existence sentences: "Kikai は arimasu" sounds like contrast or topic comment — not wrong but unusual for first mention. が is the natural choice when pointing something out.

Block C — Na-Adjectives
Unlocks vocab grid and 4-item MC.
Block C
Na-Adjectives — 5 Words
Same as i-adjectives with desu, but need な before a noun.
Vocabulary — all 5
RomajiMeaningBefore noun
shinsetsukindshinsetsu na hito
daijoubuokay / safedaijoubu na kikai
taisetsuimportanttaisetsu na shigoto
majimeserious / diligentmajime na hito
benriconvenient / usefulbenri na kikai
kireiclean / prettykirei na basho
fubeninconvenientfuben na tejun
Teaching notes
1
Write the contrast: "hayai hito" (i-adj, no extra word) vs "shinsetsu na hito" (na-adj, needs な). That is the entire difference at this level.
2
With desu both types look the same: "hayai desu / shinsetsu desu." The な only appears before a noun.
3
Point out shinsetsu and daijoubu — both appear in the reading passage coming up next. Students will see them in context.
4
MC Q4 is the key test: choose the sentence with correct な placement. Watch for students writing "majime shigoto" without な.
Watch for
⚠️
Omitting な: "shinsetsu hito" is wrong. Prompt: "Na-adjective before a noun — what goes between them?"
⚠️
Adding な with desu: "shinsetsu na desu" is non-standard. With desu, just adjective + desu.

Block D — Reading: 田中さんの一日
Unlocks the reading passage, tappable particle annotations, and comprehension MC.
Block D
田中さんの一日 — Reading + は/が Debrief
First full reading passage. Students tap particles to see why each one was chosen.
Passage — full text for reference
田中さんは DENSO で 働いています。
今日、新しい手順が あります。
手順は 難しいですから、田中さんは もう一度読みました。
機械が ステーション2にあります。
機械は 古いですから、田中さんは 監督者に伝えました。
監督者は 事務所にいます。
監督者は 親切ですから、田中さんは 安心しました。
は vs が debrief — particle by particle
SentenceParticleWhy
田中さんははTopic — introducing who the story is about
DENSOででLocation of action — where she works
手順ががNew info — procedure introduced for first time
手順ははNow topic — describing what we already know
機械ががNew info — machine introduced for first time
機械ははNow topic — describing the known machine
監督者ははTopic — supervisor enters as known participant
Talking points
1
Read the passage together first — students follow on their screens. Do not explain particles yet.
2
Then ask students to tap the particles one by one and read the annotation aloud. Let them discover the pattern before you name it.
3
Key pattern to highlight: 手順が あります → 手順は 難しい. Same noun, が introduces it, then は takes over. This is the clearest illustration in the passage.
4
Comprehension MC: 4 questions. Run through them together after reading.
5
New vocab note: で = location of action (DENSO で working), not existence. shinsetsu = na-adjective they just learned. anshin shimashita = preview only, no production expected.
Comprehension answer key
QAnswerEvidence in text
Where is the machine?Station 2Kikai ga Station 2 ni arimasu
Why read again?DifficultTejun wa muzukashii desu kara
Where is supervisor?The officeKantokusha wa jimusho ni imasu
How described?KindKantokusha wa shinsetsu desu

Block E
Everything Combined
は vs が, na-adjectives, location に, kara — all three exchanges use them together.
Exchanges at a glance
Exchange 1 — Find the supervisor (は topic, に location, shinsetsu na hito)
Exchange 2 — Report old machine (が introduces, は continues, daijoubu ja nai desu)
Exchange 3 — Explain your work to a visitor (で location of action, taisetsu, majime)
Talking points
1
Exchange 2 has daijoubu ja nai desu — negative na-adjective. Point it out: "daijoubu desu = okay. daijoubu ja nai desu = not okay." They do not need to produce it today but should recognise it.
2
Exchange 3 uses majime ni shimasu — majime + に here is adverbial (do it seriously), not the location particle. Note if it comes up.
3
Close: "Otsukaresama deshita. You just read your first Japanese paragraph and understood every particle in it."
